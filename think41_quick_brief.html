What's Missing
HERE'S WHAT'SBROKEN.

Seven gaps. Four of them critical. All of them fixable. The question is whether Think41 moves fast enough to fix them before the window closes.

Think41.com is invisible to Google
The site is a Vite/React SPA — every page renders client-side, crawlers see nothing. Zero pages indexed. Zero organic traffic. Fix: rebuild with Next.js/Gatsby SSG. Target 50+ indexed pages in 90 days.
Critical
Founders are ghosts online
Four credible, story-rich founders with near-zero regular LinkedIn presence. In B2B GenAI, the founder IS the brand signal. Fix: 2 posts/week per founder. Ghost-write if needed.
Critical
No content engine. None. Zero. Zip.
No blog. No newsletter. No editorial calendar. Zero ongoing content that builds authority, drives inbound, or keeps Think41 top-of-mind. Fix: 4 blog posts/month + newsletter + LinkedIn repurposing.
Critical
Open-source repos collecting dust
ExtraSuite and Foundation Voice are on GitHub but have no community activation layer. No Discord. No Good First Issues. No Show HN. Fix: community setup + Show HN launches for both repos.
Critical
No media or analyst relationships
Press releases hit Business Standard but no ongoing journalist or analyst relationships. Fix: identify 5 India tech journalists, brief them quarterly. Target 2 earned features in 6 months.
Significant
Four brands, zero brand architecture
ExtraSuite, Foundation Voice, Mindlap, Dev41 — no architecture tying them to Think41. Fix: "ExtraSuite by Think41" framing + unified design system across all touchpoints.
Significant
Zero events and conference presence
No speaking slots. No panels. Think41 is not in any room where enterprise buyers congregate. Fix: target SaaS Insider India, NASSCOM, Bengaluru Tech Summit. Speak — don't just exhibit.

Founder Visibility
THE FOUNDERIS THE BRAND.

In B2B GenAI, buyers don't buy from companies. They buy from people they trust. The founders are Think41's most underdeployed marketing asset.

✓ What Think41's Founders Should Do
  • Post real opinions on AI/LLMs — even contrarian ones. Especially contrarian ones.
  • Document what they're building and why in real-time
  • Share what didn't work — authenticity builds trust faster than polish
  • Speak at SaaS Insider, NASSCOM, Bengaluru tech meetups
  • Share customer outcomes with permission (even anonymised)
  • Engage comments — even one sentence reply builds community
  • Write "what we got wrong" posts — impossible to fake, impossible to ignore
✗ What Think41's Founders Should Stop Doing
  • Posting only product announcements and press releases
  • Using generic AI-generated LinkedIn content
  • Going silent for months between posts — consistency beats virality
  • Avoiding positioning stands — "we're different" needs proof, not claims
  • Leaving comments unanswered — signals disengagement
  • Over-investing in Twitter before LinkedIn is consistent
